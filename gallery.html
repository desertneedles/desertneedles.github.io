Cactus Gallery

The only place I can go to now to see cactuses up close is The Desert Pavilion at the Brooklyn Botanical Gardens. Below are some of the desert plants to be found at The Desert Pavilion

Jumping Cholla or Jumping Cactus (Scientific name: Opuntia fulgida)

It gets its name from the 3- to 8-inch joints that separate easily and can appear sometimes to "jump" to attack passersby. The Jumping Cactus is a native of the Sonoran Desert of central and south Arizona and northwest Mexico.

Toothpick Cactus (Scientific Name: Stetsonia coryne)

Found in the Low deserts of Argentina, Bolivia. It can branch up to 30 feet tall (9 m), each columnar stems is 4 inches in diameter (10 cm)

Mescal or Agave (Scientific name: Agave parryi)

The mescal plant is primarily used as a food source. Its sap is collected and fermented as a mild liquor (pulque) and can be distilled to produce mescal or tequila. Agave can be used to treat wounds, rashes, chapped lips, and sunburn.

Spurge (Scientific name: Euphorbia confinalis)

A native of South Africa, this plant has similarities to American cacti, but is found in different regions such as woodlands, near volcanos and savannas.
